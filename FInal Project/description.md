# Project Overview
Aim is to develop a quarterly turnover forecasting model designed to achieve a maximum deviation of ±5% in the first month, explicitly addressing variability in lead times and  quotation backlog for stock and production items.
The model should combine quantitative time series techniques with qualitative adjustments to enhance precision in dynamic variations, where lead time fluctuations and current quotation backlog can significantly impact turnover predictions.
A secondary objective evaluates the feasibility of linking external data sources (e.g., economic indicators, market trends) to refine forecast accuracy, especially mid and long term.
Scope: industrial production in Sweden, Finland and Norway respectively + consolidated.

# Research Problem and Motivation


Sales forecasting remains challenging due to unpredictable demand patterns and external disruptions, with traditional time series methods achieving 70-85% accuracy in stable scenarios but faltering amid variability like lead time changes or delayed orders due to external factors.
Variable lead times, arising from stock shortages, production delays or long production times introduce noise that quantitative models alone cannot mitigate, often exceeding acceptable error thresholds.
This project targets a ±5% deviation benchmark for the initial forecast month, critical for short-term inventory, cash flow, and production planning.
Additionally, it investigates external data integration to counter limitations of historical data, such as vulnerability to market shifts.

# Objectives

  *   Primary: Construct a hybrid (live data + AI) forecasting model for quarterly turnover (revenue) projections, ensuring first-month accuracy within ±5% while modeling lead time variability and probability for quotations to be switched to order.
  *   Secondary: Assess methods for incorporating external data (e.g., economic indicators, competitor activity) to improve long-range quarterly predictions.
